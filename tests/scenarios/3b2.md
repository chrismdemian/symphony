# 3B.2 Scenario — Chat ergonomics through the launcher

## Given
- `runStart` boots the launcher with a fake `MaestroProcess` and fake RPC.
- The chat panel is focused (default), the InputBar accepts typing, the message list renders empty.

## When
- The fake Maestro emits a turn that includes:
  - `assistant_text` chunks ("Looking up workers.")
  - `tool_use` for `list_workers` with `input: {}`
  - `tool_result` with ANSI-coded content `"\x1b[32mhello\x1b[0m world"`
  - more `assistant_text` ("No workers running.")
  - `turn_completed`

## Then
- The rendered frame contains:
  - `▸ list_workers` (tool summary header)
  - `hello world` (ANSI-stripped result body)
  - `Looking up workers.` and `No workers running.` (text blocks)
- The frame does NOT contain raw ANSI escape sequences (`\x1b[32m`) — `formatToolResult` stripped them.
- Exactly ONE iterator was opened by the provider (Plan §3B S3 invariant).
- A clean `handle.stop()` shuts the launcher down (LIFO chain unchanged from 3A/3B.1).

## Coverage limits
- The slash-command + `/quit` interception is covered at the unit level in `ChatPanel.test.tsx` (`intercepts /quit, calls onRequestExit, and does NOT send to Maestro`). Driving stdin keystrokes through the launcher's PassThrough doesn't decode `Enter` reliably (3B.1 audit M1 acknowledges the same limit), so /quit is NOT exercised here.
- The MessageList scrolling behavior is covered at the unit level in `MessageList.test.tsx`. `useBoxMetrics` reports zeros under `ink-testing-library`, so scenario-level scroll assertions would be vacuous.
- The `usePaste` channel is covered at the unit level via `insertChunk` in `InputBuffer.test.ts` (newline preservation is the contract). Scenario-level paste decode requires a real terminal with bracketed paste mode.
